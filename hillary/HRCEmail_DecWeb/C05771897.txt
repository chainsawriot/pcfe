                                SITUATION REPORT No. 2
                                 MGHAO2 Haiti Monitoring Group
                                             Sunday, November 28, 2010
                                                     2030 EST


PRESIDENTIAL FRONTRUNNERS CALL FOR ELECTION CANCELLATION
• (SBU) Thirteen out of 18 presidential candidates, including leading candidates Manigat and Martelly, called
for the elections to be cancelled in a joint statement. Candidates Alexis, Baker, and Ceant also denounced ballot
stuffing, restricted access for poll watchers from opposition parties, and the destruction of ballots by partisans of
the ruling party INITE. (MGHA02/Embassy Port-au-Prince telcon, CNN)
• (SBU) UN SRSG Mulet will issue a statement calling for calm and for political parties to come together to
resolve the political stalemate this evening. (MGHA02/Embassy Port-au-Prince telcon)
• (SBU) The Organization of American States (OAS) is expected to issue a statement November
29. (MGHA02/Embassy Port-au-Prince telcon)
• (SBU) Embassy Port-au-Prince observation teams report Manigat and Martelly are the lead candidates at
their observed centers. (MGHA02/Embassy Port-au-Prince telcon)

ELECTION ISSUES
• (SBU) Embassy Port-au-Prince confirmed irregularities at nine polling stations, caused largely by logistical
problems. Embassy officers did not witness ballot stuffing first-hand. Post comments it is too early to establish
how widespread the alleged irregularities are. According to the Deputy UN SRSG, 35 to 50 voting centers, out
of 1500 in total, were affected by violence. (MGHA02/Embassy Port-au-Prince telcon)
• (SBU) In Jacmel, there were reports of ballot boxes being filled with INITE ballots by 0400 EST. Some
centers have been reported closed. Voters had difficulty finding their names on voting lists, and received
inconsistent guidance from voting officials. (SOUTHCOM Mid-Day Sitrep)
• (SBU) The Conseil Electoral Provisoir closed the polling center in Corail camp after an altercation during
which IDP camp residents injured a MINUSTAH soldier with a rock. The disturbance was caused because only
39 people out of the camp population of 6000 were on the voters list. (MGHA02/Embassy Port-au-Prince
telcon)
• (SBU) In Predot, Marigot commune (South-East Department), local populations stole 900 ballot papers for
reasons unknown. (SOUTHCOM Mid-Day Sitrep)

• (SBU) Poll watchers from political parties argued at several stations over who would be allowed to enter
polling stations first, media report. Embassy Port-au-Prince comments since each of the 66 political parties is
permitted to designate poll watchers for each polling station, the polling stations could have quickly become
overcrowded. (MGHA02/Embassy Port-au-Prince telcon)

SECURITY INCIDENTS
• (SBU) Embassy Port-au-Prince reports the security situation remains generally calm. Peaceful protests have
been reported in Delmas and Petionville following the press conference by opposition presidential
candidates. (MGHA02/Embassy Port-au-Prince telcon)
• (SBU) Before scheduled quick counts could be completed, the OAS called its observers in Port-au-Prince
back to the staging area, in response to a UN travel warning issued because of the demonstrations. No security
incidents were cited. (MGHA02/Embassy Port-au-Prince telcon)
• (SBU) In Marchand Dessalines, armed men attempted to storm a voting center. Gunfire between the group
and the Haiti National Police led to one injured and two dead. Shots were also fired at polling centers in the
Artibonite and North Departments. (SOUTHCOM Mid-Day Sitrep, AFP)

CODEL CLYBURN
(SBU) CODEL Clyburn met with election observers and Ambassador Granderson, CARICOM, and visited
several polling centers. The CODEL departed at 1749. (MGHA02/Embassy Port-au-Prince telcon)

NO CONSULAR INQUIRIES
(SBU) Embassy Port-au-Prince and the Bureau of Consular Affairs report no U.S. citizens have made
inquiries. (MGHA02/Embassy Port-au-Prince telcon)
