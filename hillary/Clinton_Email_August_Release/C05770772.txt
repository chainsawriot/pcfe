AMMAN (AFP) -Jordanian Prime Minister Samir Rifai reshuffled his cabinet on July 28, dropping six ministers and
replacing them with seven others. The ministers of justice, education, agriculture, tourism, information and cabinet
affairs left the government, while Labour Minister Ibrahim Omush changed his portfolio to become state minister for
prime ministry and legal affairs. Seven new members entered the government, after they were sworn in by King
Abdullah II.
