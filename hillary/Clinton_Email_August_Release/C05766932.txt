Argentina's La   Nacion comments that the greatest lesson of events in Honduras "has been the worldwide reaction in the
name of respect for constitutional order." The paper says the military acted badly but was reacting to Zelaya's own
unconstitutional behavior, and notes that "the US was among the first to understand the complexity of the Honduran case
and the difficulties in treating it as a derivation of a classic military coup. It was also the US that was the first to announce
that it was ready to work with the Lobo government to strengthen Honduran democracy..."
