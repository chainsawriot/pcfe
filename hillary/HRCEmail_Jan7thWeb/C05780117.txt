MIDDLE EAST PROTESTS UPDATE
• (U) LIBYA: Forces loyal to Col. Qadhafi used tanks and aircraft to bombard the town of Zawiyah and
launched at least four air strikes on Ras Lanuf, with one striking a residential area. (Reuters)
• (SBU) BAHRAIN: Hardline Shiite groups Wafa', Bahrain Freedom Movement, and Mushaimaa's Haq
movement formed a coalition aimed at toppling the Sunni monarchy and setting up a republic, media
report. Embassy Manama comments the hardliners rejected the 2002 reforms and refused to participate in
parliamentary elections. The Bahrain desk adds the coalition is working to align itself more closely with
demands of street protesters.
(Ops/Embassy Manama tekon, Ops/Bahrain desk e-mail, Reuters)
• (SBU) SAUDI ARABIA: Shiites staged small protests in the Eastern Province, the source of much of the
country's oil wealth, according to press. The Saudi Arabia desk comments if media reports are accurate, the
protests would be the first since the government reiterated a longstanding ban on demonstrations. (Ops/Saudi
Arabia desk e-mail, Reuters)

OUTSPOKEN CRITIC HAITHAM MALEH RELEASED FROM SYRIAN JAIL
(SBU) The 80-year-old former judge had been on a hunger strike, according to press. The Syria desk
comments Maleh was in poor health and was released so he would not become a martyr by dying while
incarcerated. (Ops/Syria desk e-mail, Reuters)

FIGHTING IN SOUTHERN SUDAN KILLS 51
(SBU) Sudan People's Liberation Army (SPLA) soldiers attacked rebel leader George Athor's base, forcing
him to withdraw, according to press. The Office of the Special Envoy for Sudan comments SPLA forces
blocked UN soldiers from patrolling much of Jonglei State, where Athor maintained his
headquarters. (Ops/Office of the Special Envoy for Sudan e-mail, AP)
