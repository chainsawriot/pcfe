According to a number of Afghans and international observer that I have spoken to this morning, this year's
elections are thus far better than last year. Key highlights from these anecdotal observations are noted below:

Security
-While there have been a number of security attacks throughout the country, including Kandahar, Nangarhar,
Kunduz, Baghlan, Logar, Wardak, Kapisa, and Kabul thus far the number of incidents are lower than last year
(according to the                                              In Kandahar, where there were at least seven
rocket attacks, Governor Wesa's convoy struck an IED on the way to a polling center (no one was hurt).

- Pajwok News, One of Afghanistan's main paper, has cited that there are 15 dead (including 6 ANSF) and 40
injured so far. In a number of cases the incidents the cases involve candidates targeting other candidates.

Operations
- Election operations are also seen as improved over last year. IEC Manawi reported that 92% of the polling
stations were opened.
There were a few reports of complaints about the ink (claims that they were being washed off) and faulty hole
punchers.

-There were a few demonstrations in Ghazni and Kabul over lack of polling centers

Fraud
-There are many attempts at fraud but it is not systematic. The IEC also seems to be responsive and making
more of an efforts to prevent fraud. A number of people have been arrested for attempting to use fake
registration cards. In Herat over 150 fake registration cards were confiscated. In Kapisa, the governor's son was
