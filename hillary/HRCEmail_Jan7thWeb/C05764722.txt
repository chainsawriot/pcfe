                         SITUATION REPORT No. 22
                                     Honduras Task Force TFH01
                                            Monday, September 21, 2009
                                                    1730 EDT

ZELAYA RETURNS TO HONDURAS AFTER THREE MONTHS IN EXILE
(SBU) Brazilian FM Amorim confirms President Zelaya is at the Brazilian embassy, according to Embassy
Tegucigalpa. Zelaya asked for an OAS official to come to Honduras and mediate the talks between the two
parties so the San Jose Accord could be signed and implemented. In public remarks to pro-Zelaya Honduran
radio and TV stations, Zelaya praised the San Jose Accord, saying it contained all of the elements to resolve the
crisis and achieve reconciliation. He said he wants to sign the Accord, reiterating his support for its Verification
Commission. (TFH01/Embassy Tegucigalpa e-mails, Reuters)

OAS PERMANENT COUNCIL TO ISSUE STATEMENT
(SBU) USOAS reports OAS Secretary General Insulza agreed to visit Honduras to serve as a mediator. OAS
Council Chair Oyarce intends to issue a statement calling on all sectors to refrain from violence and demanding
the de facto regime respect Zelaya's physical integrity. The statement also will urge Zelaya be restored to the
presidency consistent with the San Jose Accord. (TFH01/USOAS e-mail)

PEACEFUL DEMONSTRATIONS IN TEGUCIGALPA
•    (SBU) Embassy Tegucigalpa urged de facto Minister of Security Rodas to take all possible measures to
    avoid violence. Former Honduran President Flores advised de facto Minister of Defense Sevilla to order the
    armed forces to show extreme restraint. (TFH01/Embassy Tegucigalpa e-mail)
•     (SBU) Zelaya's daughter stated thousands of supporters from the interior of the country plan to
    congregate in the capital. (TFHOVEmbassy Tegucigalpa e-mail)

EMBASSY EAC MEETS TO DISCUSS SECURITY POSTURE
•    (SBU) Embassy Tegucigalpa confirms the crowds at the Brazilian embassy, two blocks away the U.S.
    chancery, are no larger than those that have gathered since the June 28 coup. (TFHOVEmbassy
    Tegucigalpa e-mail)
•    (SBU) Post issued a warden message advising Americans to avoid non-essential travel in the
    area. (TFH01/CA-OCS e-mail)
