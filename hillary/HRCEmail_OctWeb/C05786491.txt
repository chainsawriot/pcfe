                          S/ES-0 Morning Press Highlights
                                              Tuesday, June 07, 2011
                                                   0500 EDT


                                       WESTERN HEMISPHERE
PERU/BRAZIL
President Rousseff called Peruvian President-elect Humala to congratulate him on his electoral victory, and
plans to attend Humala's July 28 inauguration, Rousseff s foreign policy advisor said. (Merco Press)

MEXICO
Troops seized two armored trucks in a machine shop in Tamaulipas state, bringing the total number seized in
the past several years to 109. The two trucks, outfitted with one-inch thick steel plates, were found with two
other partially-completed trucks and 23 tractor-trailers awaiting modification for drug-smuggling. (Latin
American Herald Tribune)

                                                   EUROPE
RUSSIA
A Russian court refused to consider a parole request from jailed tycoon Mikhail Khodorkovsky because his
lawyers failed to supply the proper documents showing he was in prison. (Reuters)

HUNGARY/ASEAN/EUROPEAN UNION
Foreign ministers from European Union and ASEAN countries that comprise the Asia-Europe Meeting (ASEM)
met in Hungary to find joint solutions to "non-traditional security challenges" such as food security, climate
change, piracy, and nuclear safety. (4FP)

                                     SOUTH AND CENTRAL ASIA
INDIA/CHINA/UNITED STATES OF AMERICA
An alleged U.S. diplomatic cable from August 2009 stated China, at the behest of Pakistan, placed a "technical
hold" on an Indian request to impose sanctions on three top leaders of Pakistan-based terrorist organizations. A
Chinese foreign ministry official said India had failed to provide sufficient information to merit the
sanctions. (The Hindu)

INDIA/FRANCE
Christine Lagarde traveled to India to meet with Prime Minister Singh as part of her campaign to overcome
emerging-economy opposition to her bid to lead the International Monetary Fund. The former French finance
minister has already visited Brazil, and plans to visit China and several African nations as well. (Dawn)

                                                   AFRICA
ZIMBABWE
A leaked version of Zimbabwe's voter rolls contains 2.6 million too many names, according to a report by a
respected South African research institute. (BBC)

SOUTH AFRICA/UNITED STATES OF AMERICA
South Africa's minister of trade, speaking ahead of the African Growth and Opportunity Act (AGOA) forum in
Lusaka, said his country will contest any decision by Washington to make radical changes to the trade
agreement, which expires in 2015. (Business Day)

                                                MIDDLE EAST
IRAN
Iranian submarines were sent to the Red Sea to collect data and identify warships of other countries, according
to Iran's semi-official news agency. (Reuters Africa)

SYRIA
A Syrian regime critic and writer of a blog titled "A Gay Girl in Damascus" was abducted from a Damascus
street while walking to a meeting. (abc.net.au)

                                         EAST ASIA AND PACIFIC
CHINA/EL SALVADOR
China's permanent observer to the Organization of the American States (OAS) Zhang Yesui told OAS General
Assembly members meeting in San Salvador that China sought deeper cooperation with OAS states, and would
provide programs for disaster prevention in Central America and water management in South
America. (People's Daily)

THE REPUBLIC OF KOREA/DEMOCRATIC PEOPLE'S REPUBLIC OF KOREA/CHINA
The Republic of Korea's (ROK) envoy to the six-party talks on the Democratic People's Republic of Korea's
(DPRK) nuclear program will fly to China to consult with his Chinese counterpart after the DPRK cut off
dialogue with the ROK. The DPRK accuses the ROK of divulging details of their secret overseas efforts to set
up a summit. (Yonhap News Agency)
