                                           SPOT REPORT
                                       The Death of Kim Jong-il
                                             Monday, December 19, 2011
                                                     0630 EST

LATEST DEVELOPMENTS
• (U) Democratic People's Republic of Korea state news agency referred to Kim Jong-il's son, Kim Jong-un,
as the "great successor." (Reuters)
• (U) Kim Jong-il's funeral will be held December 28, and Kim Jong-un will lead the National Funeral
Committee. Foreign mourning delegations will not be received. (AP, Xinhua News)
• (U) The Republic of Korea defense ministry detected no indications of unusual movement by DPRK
military. (Reuters)
• (U) A ROK/DPRK jointly-run factory park located in DPRK is operating normally according to business
officials. (Reuters)

INTERNATIONAL REACTION
• (SBU) ROK placed its military on high alert following the announcement of Kim Jong-il's death, according
to press. Embassy Seoul comments ROK President Lee convened an emergency meeting of the National
Security Council to review the internal and external security situation and ensure appropriate response measures
were in place.
(Ops/Embassy Seoul e-mail, Reuters)
• (U) ROK President Lee called for calm following the announcement of Kim Jong-il's death, asking residents
of the country to carry on with daily life. (Reuters)
• (SBU) China offered its deep condolences on the death of Kim Jong-il, media report. Embassy Beijing
comments ROK diplomats in Beijing have not been in contact yet with Chinese officials. (Ops/Embassy
Beijing e-mail, Xinhua News)
• (SBU) The Japanese government expressed their condolences on the death of Kim Jong-il, and PM Noda
cancelled a speaking engagement, according to press. Embassy Tokyo comments Noda instructed ministers to
monitor the situation carefully and be ready to respond to developments. (Ops/Embassy Tokyo e-mail,
Telegraph)
