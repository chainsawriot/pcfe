                                                  MIDDLE EAST
ISRAEL/PALESTINIAN AUTHORITY
The High Court of Justice rejected petitions against an amendment to the citizenship law, preventing the
unification of Palestinians with their spouses in Israel. (Jerusalem Post)

LEBANON/ISRAEL
The Lebanese Armed Forces arrested a man suspected of spying for Israel since 1977 by giving it access to the
Lebanese communications system through which the Palestinian Liberation Organization sent messages to its
operatives. (Met News)

                                                   AFRICA

SOMALIA/AFRICAN UNION
The African Union called on the UN Security Council to increase the upper limit for peacekeepers in Somalia
from 12,000 to 17,731 and also for greater financial help for AU forces in the country. (AFP)

MADAGASCAR/SOUTH AFRICA
A senior aide to former Malagasy President Ravalomanana accused South Africa of "double standards" after the
South African government failed to honor a 2010 African Union travel ban on 2009 coup leader Andry
Rajoelina when he attended January celebrations in Bloemfontein. (Business Day)

                                     SOUTH AND CENTRAL ASIA

AFGHANISTAN
President Karzai pledged to take action against the "cowardly" perpetrators of violence against women in the
wake of a case of torture against a child bride. (Khaleej Times)

BANGLADESH
Bangladesh arrested former head of the opposition Jamaat-e-Islami party, Ghulam Azam, on charges of war
crimes during the 1971 liberation struggle against Pakistan. (The Times of India)

                                        EAST ASIA AND PACIFIC
DEMOCRATIC PEOPLE'S REPUBLIC OF KOREA
• In a letter to a Japanese newspaper, Kim Jong-nam, eldest brother of DPRK leader Kim Jong-un, expressed
doubts about the leader's hold on power, anticipating the ruling elite will extend their influence over the
country. (Yonhap News Agency)
• The body of former leader Kim Jong-il will lie in state permanently at a mausoleum and his February
birthday will be instituted as the "Day of the Shining Star." (Korea Herald)

AUSTRALIA/JAPAN
Anti-whaling activists renewed attempts to disable a harpoon ship in the Japanese fleet, as the conflict stretched
across a wide swath of the Southern Ocean. (Sydney Morning Herald)

                                                   EUROPE

RUSSIA
United Russia Duma Deputy Skotch drafted a bill requiring officials to declare their expenses, though critics
dismissed it as effectively toothless unless Russia ratifies Article 20 of the UN Convention against
Corruption. (The Moscow Times)

UNITED KINGDOM
An amendment presented by Scottish MP Salmond requests the support of Scottish Parliament as he seeks an
independence referendum in the autumn of 2014. (BBC)

                                         WESTERN HEMISPHERE
ARGENTINA/UNITED KINGDOM
Victory Front lawmaker Carlos Kunkel said the United Kingdom's "aggression and piracy continues," and
Argentina "must be politically persistent while maintaining a constant defense" on its claims over the
sovereignty of the Falkland Islands. (Buenos Aires Herald)

MEXICO/GUATEMALA
Mexican immigration officials said they returned 70 Guatemalan squatters on a communal farm to their
country. (Houston Chronicle)
