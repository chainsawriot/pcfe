                                         WESTERN HEMISPHERE
CANADA
Toronto-born Omar Khadr, the youngest detainee ever held at Guantanamo Bay, officially requested to return to
Canada. (Montreal Gazette)

JAMAICA/CHINA
A Chinese navy hospital ship carrying more than 100 medical volunteers who will provide free surgery, CAT
scans, eye care and other procedures, landed in Jamaica. (Miami Herald)

A proposed referendum would allow millions of Turks living abroad to cast their votes from outside the country
in the next presidential and general elections. (Hurriyet Daily News)

RUSSIA
Russian President Medvedev will attend the G20 summit in Cannes and will hold bilateral meetings with his
counterparts, including the Chinese and Brazilian presidents. (RIA Novosti)

                                               MIDDLE EAST
ISRAEL/IRAN
Prime Minister Netanyahu and Defense Minister Barak are trying to bring together a majority in the cabinet in
favor of military action against Iran. (Haaretz)

EGYPT
The majority of political party representatives who attended a meeting with Deputy Prime Minister for Political
Affairs Al Selmy rejected Article 9 of the draft constitutional principles document under discussion, which
guarantees the secrecy of the armed forces budget. (Daily News Egypt)

                                     SOUTH AND CENTRAL ASIA
PAKISTAN
Pakistan will not accept the final $3.7 billion tranche of an International Monetary Fund loan package after
rejecting the organization's strict reform demands. (The Daily Times)

INDIA
Maoist rebels kidnapped 15 employees and contract workers of a private construction company in India's
eastern state of Bihar. (BBC)

                                        EAST ASIA AND PACIFIC
AUSTRALIA
Australia's government introduced legislation to impose a 30 percent tax on the burgeoning profits of iron ore
and coal mining companies that are feeding Chinese demand. (Sydney Morning Herald)

THAILAND
Floodwater advanced deeper into Bangkok from its northern outskirts as a northern sluice gate and a dyke failed
to limit inflows to the inner city. (Bangkok Post)

                                                    AFRICA
GUINEA/SENEGAL
Senegal banned all planes traveling to or from Guinea from using its airspace following a dispute over the
grounding of a Senegalese plane in Guinea. (News 24)

NIGERIA
Nigeria ordered an investigation into illegal oil bunkering, an industry believed to be worth hundreds of
millions of dollars a year. Nigeria's production capacity has been slashed by thieves drilling into pipelines that
pass through the vast Niger Delta region. (IOL News)
