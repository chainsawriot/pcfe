                                                       EUROPE
TURKEY/QATAR
Qatar's Emir Hamad visited Turkish Prime Minister Erdogan in Istanbul. The prime minister's office
subsequently announced Erdogan cancelled a planned trip to Qatar. (TodaysZaman)

RUSSIA
NATO Secretary General Rasmussen said Russian President Medvedev's remarks about Russian
countermeasures against the European missile defense system might be election rhetoric, contrary to President
Medvedev's assertion they were not. Rasmussen added he was optimistic about the Russia-NATO summit
planned for May 2012. (RIA Novosti)

                                                  MIDDLE EAST
ISRAEL
Interior Minister Yishai said the approximately 50,000 African migrants in Israel are "infiltrators" with no
political standing to seek asylum, and he will work to expel them from Israel. (Jerusalem Post)

PALESTINIAN AUTHORITY/UNITED NATIONS
UNESCO announced the Palestinian Authority's flag will be raised at UNESCO's Paris headquarters December
13. (Dawn)

                                                     AFRICA
KENYA/SOMALIA/AFRICAN UNION
The Kenyan parliament unanimously voted to authorize the Kenyan Defense Forces in Somalia to serve under
the AU Mission to Somalia. (All Africa)

SOUTH AFRICA/AUSTRALIA
At the UN Climate Change Conference in Durban, Australian Climate Change Minister Combet announced
Australia will provide $25 million to help African countries adapt manage water resources and boost food
security. (Sydney Morning Herald)

                                      SOUTH AND CENTRAL ASIA
INDIA
President Patil hailed five girls who resisted child marriage as "icons" and presented each with a cash
award. (The Hindu)

PAKISTAN/IRAN
Iranian security forces shot at Pakistani fishermen when their vessel entered Iranian waters, killing three and
injuring two. The dead and injured men are in Iranian custody. (The Express Tribune)

                                         EAST. ASIA AND PACIFIC
JAPAN/CHINA
Prime Minister Noda canceled his trip to Beijing at the request of Chinese officials, who expressed concern
anti-Japanese sentiment might increase around the December 13 anniversary of the 1937 Nanjing
Massacre. (The Japan Times)

PHILIPPINES/CAMBODIA
Cambodia and the Philippines signed an agreement in Phnom Penh to boost bilateral cooperation on trade,
investment, tourism, and agriculture. (The Philippine Star)

                                         WESTERN HEMISPHERE
TRINIDAD AND TOBAGO/CARICOM/CUBA
Ten heads of state of the Caribbean Community (CARICOM) and Cuban President Castro will meet in Port of
Spain December 8. (Caribbean News Now)

MEXICO
Mexican prosecutors announced three men allied with the Zeta drug cartel were arrested for the November
murder of 26 people in Guadalajara. (Miami Herald)
