ARAB MONITORS END FIRST DAY OF OBSERVATION IN SYRIA
(U) Amidst reports Syrian forces dispersed large numbers of protesters with tear gas, the head of the Arab
League said the team's first day in Horns was "very good" with all sides being responsive to the
monitors. (Reuters)

SUPPORTERS OF PAKISTANI PRESIDENT RALLY AT BHUTTO'S GRAVE
(SBU) President Zardari used the fourth anniversary of his wife's death to assure a crowd of 70,000 supporters
he would not resign, according to press. The Pakistan desk comments Zardari sidestepped criticizing the
military, but confronted the Supreme Court on insufficient progress in Bhutto's assassination
case. (Ops/Pakistan desk telcon, Reuters)

EGYPT BANS VIRGINITY TESTS FOR FEMALE DETAINEES
(SBU) An administrative court issued the ruling after a woman sued Egypt's military-led government after
being subjected to such a test in March, media report. Embassy Cairo comments the ruling comes as Egyptian
women are increasingly calling for the prosecution of those responsible for recent assaults on women in Tahrir
Square.
(Ops/Embassy Cairo e-mail, CNN)

TURKEY, AZERBAIJAN SIGN PROPOSAL TO TRANSIT GAS TO EUROPE
(SBU) The Office of the Special Envoy for Eurasian Energy comments the proposed $5 billion natural gas
pipeline could serve as a building block for a larger volume pipeline system to bring gas to southeastern and
central Europe. Embassy Ankara comments the agreement signals the State Oil Company of Azerbaijan is
serious about moving expeditiously to set up the southern gas corridor, an initiative of the European
Commission to supply gas to Europe. The Bureau of European Affairs adds the agreement could provide the
basis for a scalable Nabucco pipeline, the previously proposed natural gas pipeline backed by the EU.
(Ops/Embassy Ankara e-mail, Ops/S/EEE e-mail, Ops/EUR e-mail)
