                                                 MGAGO1
                                           Saturday, January 19, 2013
                                                   1630 EST

LATEST DEVELOPMENTS
• (U) The Algerian interior ministry stated the final assault today resulted in the death of 11 militants and
seven hostages. During the three-day military operation to end the crisis, 32 militants and 23 hostages were
killed. A total of 685 Algerian and 107 foreign workers were freed over the course of the standoff. (AP)
• (U) The Algerian army is clearing mines left by the militants at the In Amenas Central Processing
Facility. (AP)
• (U) Algerian special forces found 15 burned bodies near the Central Processing Facility. Identity and cause
of death are still unknown. (Reuters)
• (U) The head of Norway's Statoil stated 12 employees were safe and the fate of five more unknown. (CNN
Arabic)
• (SBU) UK PM Cameron said one British citizen was killed and five are unaccounted for, media report. The
priority of the UK is to get their citizens home quickly and look after survivors. Embassy London comments
the British Ambassador to Algeria traveled to In Amenas along with political staff British investigative staff
have not yet arrived. (MGAGOVEmbassy London e-mail, BBC)
• (U) Romanian PM Ponta stated a Romanian hostage had been freed and is being cared for by the Algerian
army. (AFP)
• (U) Japanese PM Abe said he had received dire information about Japanese hostages but did not provide
further detail. (AFP)
• (U) Colombian President Santos said it was highly likely a Colombian national is among the
victims. (BBC)
• (U) Algerian state owned oil company Sonatrach stated Algeria was losing $11 million per day as a result of
the shutdown of the In Amenas plant. (Reuters)
• (U) BP Chief Executive Dudley claimed the militants intended to destroy the entire plant. (Telegraph)

CONSULAR UPDATE
• (SBU) Mr. Buttaccio's remains arrived at Dover AFB at 1009 EST. (MGAGOVNMCC e-mail and tekon)

INTERNATIONAL REACTION
• (U) French President Hollande said the Algerian response to the crisis was appropriate, as negotiations with
the militants were not possible. (Reuters)
• (U) Libyan PM Zeidan stated Libya would not allow the country to be used as a base for attacking its
neighbors. (Reuters)
