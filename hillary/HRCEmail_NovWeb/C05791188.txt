ADDIS ABABA, July 15 (Reuters) - Doctor and diplomat Nkosazana Dlamini-Zuma of South Africa won a
vote on Sunday to become the first female head of the African Union Commission, delegates said.

   The ex-wife of South African President Jacob Zuma defeated incumbent and rival Jean Ping of Gabon, who
had headed the steering body of the 54-member organisation since 2008.
