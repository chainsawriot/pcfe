FOREIGN REACTION
• (U) In an interview with CNN Turk, Foreign Minister Davutoglu downplayed possible damage to the U.S.-
Turkish relationship, stating the two countries have a very close dialogue at every level. He cited his upcoming
visit to Washington to meet the Secretary and National Security Advisor as an example. (Rapid Response Unit
Special Report on WikiLeaks)
• (U) Russia's Interfax quotes Foreign Minister Lavrov as blaming the leaks on "little thieves running around
the internet." (AFP)

MEDIA REACTION
• (U) UK's Daily Mail headlines "U.S. Warns World Leaders that New Revelations Will Expose 'Corruption
between Allies' and Show What America Really Thinks of Its Friends." The paper alleges the "bombshell leak"
will include assessments of former Prime Minister Brown's personality and secret discussions of returning the
Lockerbie bomber to Libya. (The Daily Mail)
• (U) Spain's El Correo comments, "Starting this weekend, U.S. diplomacy will become a lot more difficult.
These documents could potentially demonstrate hypocrisy in U.S. policy." (Rapid Response Unit Special
Report on WikiLeaks)
• (U) Former German Ambassador to the United States Wolfgang Ischinger strongly defends the importance
of some issues remaining secret in diplomatic communications in an interview in the left-leaning Berliner
Zeitung. (WWG01/Embassy Berlin e-mail)
• (U) Pakistan's Geo TV claims WikiLeaks will post 94 secret documents about Pakistan, some of which
include discussions of Pakistan's policy toward Afghanistan, the debate within Pakistan on the war against
terrorism, U.S.-Pakistani military and intelligence cooperation, and U.S. reservations about Pakistan's nuclear
program.
(Rapid Response Unit Special Report on WikiLeaks)
• (U) Arabic-language papers in Bahrain and Kuwait run U.S. Ambassador to Iraq James Jeffrey's comments:
"We are worried. WikiLeaks are an absolutely awful impediment to my business. They will not help; they will
simply hurt our ability to do our work."
(Rapid Response Unit Special Report on WikiLeaks, AFP)
• (U) Palestinian papers report world capitals are preparing for the "WikiLeaks storm" and "worst case
scenarios." (Rapid Response Unit Special Report on WikiLeaks)
• (U) Analysts in Greece's Ethnos speculate about potential expulsions of diplomats as a result of the
disclosures. (Rapid Response Unit Special Report on WikiLeaks)
