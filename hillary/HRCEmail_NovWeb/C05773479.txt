HAITIAN PRESIDENT SIGNS SWEEPING CONSTITUTIONAL REFORM
(SBU) President Martelly signed amendments providing for dual nationality, 30 percent representation of
women in public service and political parties, the creation of a Constitutional Council, and the implementation
of a method to select a permanent electoral council. The President's act follows a 13-month impasse after
several errors were noted in the version published by former President Preval in May 2011. President Martelly
followed the proper legal procedure when signing the amendments, allowing the document to become
law. (Ops/Embassy Port-au-Prince telcon and e-mail)

PAKISTAN SUPREME COURT DISQUALIFIES PM GILANI
(SBU) Embassy Islamabad comments a three-member bench barred PM Gilani from holding office as of April
26, the date of his conviction for contempt of court. The Election Commission of Pakistan has notified Gilani
of his disqualification, and leaders of ruling coalition political parties are meeting to decide who will succeed
him. The leading candidates are Textiles Minister Shahabuddin and Water and Power Minister
Mukhtar. (Ops/Embassy Islamabad e-mail, Reuters)

CHARLES TAYLOR TO APPEAL WAR CRIMES CONVICTION
(SBU) Embassy The Hague comments the prosecution also indicated its intent to appeal the judgment, which
only convicted former Liberian President Taylor of abetting crimes against humanity. Once the pretrial judge
makes certain preliminary decisions on timing and logistics, which could take up to five weeks, both sides will
file formal notices of intention to appeal the judgment. (Ops/Embassy The Hague e-mail, AP)
