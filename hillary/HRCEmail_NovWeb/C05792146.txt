                                         EAST ASIA AND PACIFIC
AUSTRALIA/INDONESIA
The 54 asylum-seekers rescued when their boat sank August 29 off Java will be transferred to Indonesia instead
of to Australia, in a move reflecting the Australian government's toughening border protection policy. (The
Australian)

COOK ISLANDS/NEW ZEALAND
At the Pacific Islands Forum in the Cook Islands, New Zealand Prime Minister Key pledged an extra $10
million in aid to help protect and manage Pacific fisheries and oceans. (3 News)

                                       SOUTH AND CENTRAL ASIA
INDIA/IRAN/PAKISTAN
During a meeting between Indian Prime Minister Singh and Pakistani President Zardari on the margins of the
Non-Aligned Movement (NAM) summit, India agreed to allow a judicial commission from Pakistan to cross-
examine three witnesses related to the trial of November 2008 terrorist Ajmal Kasab. (Hindustan Times)

PAKISTAN/IRAN
During a meeting at the NAM summit, Iranian President Ahmadinejad and Pakistani President Zardari agreed to
implement bilateral mega-projects, such as an Iran-Pakistan gas pipeline, electricity transmission lines, and
transportation links. (Dawn)

                                                MIDDLE EAST
EGYPT
Several Egyptian groups called for a march in Cairo to demand a trial for all Egyptian military members
involved in the 2011 killing of protestors, the rejection of IMF and World Bank loan proposals, and the release
of political prisoners. (Abram Online)

PALESTINIAN AUTHORITY/ISRAEL/UNITED NATIONS
Foreign Minister Maliki said Palestinian Authority President Abbas will not ask the UN General Assembly to
recognize a Palestinian state next month. (Jerusalem Post)

                                                     AFRICA
ERITREA
Media watchdog Reporters Without Borders confirmed four journalists died in prison after being held for
several years. (News 24)

UGANDA/IRAN
During the NAM summit in Tehran, President Museveni criticized Western intervention in developing
countries, saying it interfered with their development and that of local leaders. (Daily Monitor)

                                         WESTERN HEMISPHERE
ARGENTINA/FALKLAND ISLANDS (MALVINAS)/UNITED KINGDOM
The Falkland Islands Executive Council officially approved plans for a March 2013 referendum to be held on
the islands' political status. (Merco Press)

COLOMBIA
Dockworkers paralyzed operations at Colombia's main Pacific port by striking to demand higher salaries, direct
work contracts, and an end to abusive sub-contracting practices. (Latin American Herald Tribune)

                                                    EUROPE
RUSSIA
An opposition group applied to hold a third "March for Millions" September 15 to demand the release of
political prisoners. (RIA Novosti)

SPAIN
Authorities evacuated approximately 1000 people from the resort of Marbella on Spain's Costa del Sol because
of a large wildfire (BBC)
