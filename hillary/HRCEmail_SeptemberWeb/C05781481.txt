SAUDI WOMEN AGAIN CHALLENGE DRIVING BAN


(SBU) Dozens of Saudi women took to the roads June 17 as part of a social media campaign, media report. The Saudi
Arabia desk notes no women were detained. Embassy Riyadh comments the organizers called for women to continue
the campaign. The Saudi government characterizes the driving prohibition as a religious, not legal issue, and religious
scholars are now debating the validity of the 1991 religious decree which formed the basis of the ban. (Ops/Saudi
Arabia desk telcon, Ops/Embassy Riyadh telcon, AP, The New York Times)

KING DECLARES MOROCCO CONSTITUTIONAL MONARCHY
(SBU) The new constitution, put to a referendum July 1, elevates the prime minister to head of the government
and ensures he is selected by the party receiving the most votes in the election, rather than chosen by the king,
media report. King Mohammed VI remains commander of the army and highest religious authority. The
Morocco desk comments the king would still retain a significant amount of control, and it is unclear how some
of the recommendations would be implemented and enforced. Embassy Rabat comments public reaction
ranged from celebration and support to skepticism and doubt.
(Ops/Morocco desk e-mail, Ops/Embassy Rabat telcon, AP)

PORTUGUESE PM-DESIGNATE NAMES NEW FINANCE MINISTER
(SBU) As a director with the European Commission, new Finance Minister Vitor Gaspar was tasked with
implementing austerity measures for Portugal from the EU and the IMF, according to press. Embassy Lisbon
comments Gaspar is a well-regarded, experienced economist. With this choice, Prime Minister-designate
Coelho is reassuring his EU partners, the IMF, and the markets his government will move quickly to implement
necessary reforms.
(Ops/Embassy Lisbon telcon and e-mail, Reuters)
