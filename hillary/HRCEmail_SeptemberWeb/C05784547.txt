         Muhammad Ali, Other Prominent U.S. Muslims to Plea
            for Release of American Hikers Jailed in Iran

    WASHINGTON — American icon and three-time World Heavyweight Boxing
    Champion Muhammad Ali and other prominent American Muslims and clergy will join
    the families of the two U.S. hikers detained in Iran to appeal to Iran's Supreme Leader to
    release the American hikers on the Islamic foundation of mercy, forgiveness and
    compassion.

    A media conference will be held in the Holeman Lounge of the National Press Club on
    Tuesday, May 24 at 10:00 a.m. The event is open to media and coverage is welcome.
    Photo and video opportunities will be available.

     Shane Bauer and Josh Fattal have been detained in Iran since July 31, 2009. A third
     hiker, Sarah Shourd was released on September 14, 2010. Ms. Shourd will also be in
     attendance.

    Who:              Muhammad Ali, Three-time World Heavyweight Boxing Champion

                      Imam Mohamed Magid, President of the Islamic Society of North
                      America

                      Dr. Akbar Ahmed, Ibn Khaldun Chair of Islamic Studies at American
                      University

                      Dr. Parvez Shah, secretary general of the Universal Muslim Association
                      of America

                      Sarah Shourd, former detainee

                      Laura Fattal, mother of detained hiker Josh Fattal

                      Alex Fattal, brother of detained hiker Josh Fattal

                      Cindy Hickey, mother of detained hiker Shane Bauer

                      Al Bauer, father of detained hiker Shane Bauer

     What:            American icon and three-time World Heavyweight Boxing Champion
                      Muhammed Ali and other prominent American Muslims and clergy will
                      join the families of the two U.S. hikers jailed in Iran to appeal to Iran's
                      Supreme Leader to release the American hikers on the Islamic foundation
                      of mercy, forgiveness and compassion.
