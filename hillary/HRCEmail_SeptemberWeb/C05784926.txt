PALESTINIANS LOSE TRUST IN QUARTET REPRESENTATIVE BLAIR
(SBU) The Palestinian Authority said it was set to break off contact with Blair, accusing him of a pro-Israel
bias, according to press. Consulate General Jerusalem comments this sentiment has been building for some
time, but spilled over into the public domain as a result of the September 23 Quartet statement. The PLO
Executive Committee will meet September 29 to discuss the issue. (Ops/ConGen Jerusalem e-mail,
Telegraph)

PRO-KURDISH PARTY ENDS BOYCOTT OF TURKISH PARLIAMENT
(SBU) The Peace and Democracy Party (BDP) deputies, who had refused to take their parliamentary oaths in
June to protest the continued imprisonment of six colleagues, will join parliament October 1, media
report. Embassy Ankara comments the BDP's return signals the party's conclusion its cause would be better
served by working within the system, particularly given the upcoming process to rewrite Turkey's
constitution. (Ops/Embassy Ankara e-mail, Hurriyet Daily News)

CHINA AND SOUTH AFRICA AGREE TO ENHANCED COOPERATION
(SBU) The Chinese and South African vice presidents signed deals on mineral resources and finance in
Beijing, according to press. Embassy Pretoria comments South Africa is particularly focused on financing its
infrastructure investment program. Embassy Beijing adds China might be promoting increased economic
cooperation to influence South Africa's decision on whether to issue a visa to the Dalai Lama. (Ops/Embassy
Pretoria e-mail, Ops/Embassy Beijing e-mail, Xinhua News)

DUTCH TO PARTICIPATE IN NATO MISSILE SHIELD
(SBU) The Netherlands will deploy four ships with modernized radar systems at a cost of $135 to $340 million,
media report. The U.S. Mission to NATO comments the contribution is an important sign of the Dutch
commitment to NATO, particularly given significant cuts in Dutch defense spending. Embassy The Hague
adds the decision to participate was palatable to the public as the system is defensive in nature. (Ops/Embassy
The Hague tekon, Ops/USNATO e-mail, NIS News)
