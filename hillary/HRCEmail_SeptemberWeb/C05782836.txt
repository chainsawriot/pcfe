BURMA TO RELEASE 6300 PRISONERS
(SBU) The announcement followed an open letter written by Win Mra, chairman of the government-appointed
National Human Rights Commission, urging President Sein to release prisoners of conscience who did not
threaten the stability of the state, media report. Embassy Rangoon comments while news of the amnesty was
welcomed, prisoner advocacy groups emphasize it is unclear how many political prisoners will be included until
releases begin October 12. (Ops/Embassy Rangoon telcon and e-mail, AP, Reuters)

ROK OPPOSITION FIGURE WANTS RENEGOTIATED FREE TRADE DEAL
(SBU) Democratic Party leader Sohn Hak-kyu said the agreement was unacceptable in its current form because
it unfairly favored the U.S. auto industry, according to press. Embassy Seoul comments it is unlikely Sohn
would actually block ratification if the U.S. Congress passes the treaty. (Ops/Embassy Seoul e-mail, Reuters)

LEBANESE SPECIAL TRIBUNAL PRESIDENT RESIGNS
(SBU) Antonio Cassese served as the president of the Special Tribunal for Lebanon (STL) since 2009 and will
be replaced by New Zealand judge Sir David Baragwanath, media report. Embassy The Hague comments the
resignation was.not expected until early 2012 and is most likely due to illness. As Baragwanath does not have
much international tribunal experience, Cassese might continue to exert influence on the STL because he will
remain an appeals judge for the tribunal. (Ops/Embassy The Hague telcon and e-mail, The Daily Star)

MEXICAN DRUG-RELATED DEATHS SURPASS 10,000 IN 2011
