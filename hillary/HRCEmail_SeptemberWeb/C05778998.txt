                                 SITUATION REPORT No. 2
                                        Situation in Egypt TFEGO1
                                                Sunday, January 30,2011
                                                       0600 EST

LATEST DEVELOPMENTS
• (U) President Mubarak reportedly left Cairo for the safety of Sharm el-Sheikh in the Sinai. (Haaretz)
• (U) The death toll from the protests has risen to at least 150. Demonstrations continue throughout the
country. (Al Jazeera)
• (SBU) Embassy Cairo notes the Ministry of Interior has little presence in the streets. Police stations across
the country have been ransacked and burned, with looters stealing weapons. At least two majotprisons, one
close to the Embassy residential neighborhood of Maadi, and one in Fayoum, have been abandoned. Prisoners
overpowered guards and are now armed, according to press. (TFEGO1 Interagency telcon, Al Jazeera)
• (U) Egypt suspended Al Jazeera's operations in the country. (Reuters)
• (U) Egypt closed its Rafah border with the Palestinian territory of Gaza. (AP)

AUTHORIZED VOLUNTARY DEPARTURE AND TRAVEL WARNING
• (SBU) The State Department issued a travel warning to American citizens and authorized voluntary
departure of non-emergency U.S. government personnel and dependents.
(TFEGO1 Interagency telcon)
• (SBU) The United States is organizing charter flights to bring Amcits and immediate relatives to locations in
Europe. Initial flights are expected to begin January 31.
(TFEGO1 Interagency telcon)
• (SBU) The F-77 report estimates over 50,000 Amcits in Egypt. Embassy Cairo notes high demand for
assistance in departing the country. (F-77 report, TFEGO1 Interagency telcon)

• (SBU) Embassy Cairo confirmed Amcits departed from Cairo International Airport safely January
29. Consular and RSO staff will attempt to assess airport operations and security January 30. (TFEGO1
Interagency telcon)
• (SBU) Embassy Cairo noted heavy call volume from Amcits concerned about violence near their homes in
Cairo and Alexandria. There have been no reports of Amcits injured.
(TFEGO1 interagency telcon)
•, (SBU) The Bureau of Consular Affairs reports the volume of inquiries increased dramatically upon press
reports the embassy is urging Americans to leave the country.. (TFEG01/TFEGO2)

INTERNATIONAL REACTIONS
• (SBU) Japanese Foreign Minister Maehara requested close coordination with the United States in assisting
Japanese citizens in Egypt. (TFEG01/Embassy Tokyo telcon)
• (U) India has made arrangements to airlift Indian nationals out of Egypt. (The Hindu)
• (U) Prime Minister Gillard urged Australians to leave Egypt. The Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade
upgraded its travel advisory to the highest "do not travel" category.
(Sydney Morning Herald)
• (U) Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu said he hoped stability and peaceful ties with Egypt would
continue. (Reuters)
• (U) Turkey is sending two planes to evacuate its citizens January 30. (Reuters)
