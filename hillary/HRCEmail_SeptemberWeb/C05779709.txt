MIDDLE EAST PROTESTS UPDATE
• (SBU) LIBYA: Four C-130 aircrafts transported approximately 330 Egyptian passengers and three
embedded NBC journalists from Djerba to Cairo.
(Ops/Embassy Tunis e-mail, Ops/Embassy Cairo e-mail)
• (U) LIBYA: Rebels repelled an attack by pro-Qadhafi forces on the town of Misrata, 125 miles east of
Tripoli. Pro-Qadhafi troops retook Bin Jawad and conducted airstrikes on Ras Lanuf. (Reuters, AP)
• (U) LIBYA: UK Foreign Secretary Hague said a small British team detained in Benghazi had left the
county. (Reuters)
• (SBU) EGYPT: Prime Minister Sharaf announced his new transitional cabinet on his official Facebook
page. Embassy Cairo comments new Foreign Minister Nabil al-Araby is a well-regarded professional diplomat
who was an early supporter of the Tahrir Square protesters.
 (Ops/Embassy Cairo e-mail, Cairo 332)
• (U) EGYPT: Witnesses claimed soldiers fired into the air and unidentified men confronted protesters with
knives during a demonstration outside Interior Ministry offices in Cairo. (Reuters)
• (SBU) BAHRAIN: A group of nonaligned youth is planning a sit-in protest near Embassy Manama March
7. Post notes major human rights organizations and political societies have denied organizing or endorsing this
protest, which was organized via SMS messages. (Ops/Embassy Manama telcon and e-mail)
• (SBU) BAHRAIN: An estimated 10,000 Shiite opposition supporters blocked the entrance to the prime
minister's office, but failed to disrupt a government meeting, according to press. Embassy Manama comments
the protest was peaceful and attended by the secretaries general of the Wifaq and Wa'ad political societies.
(Ops/Embassy Manama telcon and e-mail, AP)
