FUKUSHIMA DAI-ICHI NUCLEAR ISSUES
• (SBU) TEPCO reported one additional worker was exposed to unsafe levels of radiation, bringing the total
number of TEPCO employees exposed to dangerous levels to seven. (7'FJP01/Embassy Tokyo email)
• (SBU) Aerial monitoring flights conducted March 19 showed the 50-mile evacuation zone remains
adequate. Air samples do not indicate any further melting of the reactor cores.
(DOE sitrep)
• (SBU) At the request of Japanese and U.S. governments, the Australian military plans to move one pump
from Perth to Japan on March 21 for cooling the reactors. (TFJP01)
• (SBU) The IAEA began measuring radiation in air and soil in the Tokyo metropolitan area March 19. The
IAEA also asked the United States to share detailed measurement data. (TFJP01/Embassy Tokyo email,
L4EA/TFJP01 fax)
• (SBU) TEPCO decided there is no immediate need to vent the pressure at the Unit 3 reactor. The pressure is
relatively high, but it has stabilized. (TFJP01/Embassy Tokyo email, AP)
• (U) Engineers restored power to the Unit 2 reactor. (Reuters)
• (U) Japan's Self Defense Forces (SDF) sprayed 80 tons of water to cool the storage pool for spent nuclear
fuel at the Unit 4 reactor. (NHK World)
• (SBU) Contrary to previous reports, NRC representatives in Tokyo now assess Unit 3 and the spent fuel pool
at Unit 4 to have water. (TFJP01/Embassy Tokyo conference call)
• (SBU) Units 5 and 6 were ventilated March 19 to prevent hydrogen buildup from causing an explosion. The
temperature at both buildings decreased, indicating cooling is working again. (TFJP01/Embassy Tokyo email)
• (U) TEPCO stated the radiation level within the Fukushima facility declined since water spraying began
March 19. (NHK online)

CONSULAR ISSUES AND POST OPERATIONS
• (SBU) Consulate General Naha reported that DOD dependents are authorized to take commercial flights in
addition to DOD charters. (TFJP01/Consulate Naha phone call)
• (SBU) U.S. law firm Jones Day closed its Tokyo office and is relocating 110 attorneys, staff, and their
families to southwest Japan. (TFJP01/Embassy Tokyo email)

INTERNATIONAL ASSISTANCE AND RESPONSE
• (u) The National Police Agency reported 8,199 dead and 12,722 missing. (AP)
• (SBU) Japanese Ambassador Fujisaki and a USAID representative greeted the Fairfax County USAR team
upon arrival in the United States at 0415 EDT. (TFJP01/USAID phone call)
• (U) Japan announced the creation of a task force under the prime minister's office for improved coordination
of humanitarian assistance. (TFJP01/Embassy Tokyo conference call)
• (SBU) Japanese SDF delivered more than seven tons of kerosene airlifted by U.S. military helicopters to
Kusennuma City, Miyagi prefecture. (TFJP01/Embassy Tokyo email)
