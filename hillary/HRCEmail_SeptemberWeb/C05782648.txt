                                                  MIDDLE EAST
LIBYA/UNITED KINGDOM
UK company Heritage Oil PLC paid $19.5 million for a 51 percent stake in Libyan oilfield service provider
Sahara Oil Services Holdings Ltd. Heritage said it wants to play a significant role in Libya's oil and gas
industry. (Financial Times)

BAHRAIN
A court handed life sentences to 14 defendants charged with killing a Pakistani man in March. Members of the
Pakistani community in Manama were targeted during the spring's unrest. (Gulf News)

                                        SOUTH AND CENTRAL ASIA
BANGLADESH
The International Crimes Tribunal indicted opposition leader Moulana Sayedee for crimes against humanity
during the 1971 Liberation War. Sayedee pleaded not guilty to the charges, which include rape, murder, arson,
looting, and forcing Hindus to convert to Islam. (Bangladesh Today)

AFGHANISTAN/PAKISTAN
The Taliban released eight Pakistani aid workers who had been abducted from Pishin in July. This was the
fourth kidnapping near Quetta in recent months. (Pakistan Tribune)

                                                   AFRICA
BOTSWANA/KENYA/RWANDA/SOUTH AFRICA/TANZANIA/UGANDA/ZIMBABWE
A new report warns the increased number of women choosing long-lasting contraceptive injections could be
helping to spread HIV. Researchers followed 3800 couples in seven African countries worst hit by the AIDS
epidemic. (The Guardian)

CAMEROON
A Commonwealth Expert Team arrived in Yaounde October 3 to observe the October 9 presidential
elections. The team, led by former Foreign Affairs Minister for the Bahamas Frederick Mitchell, will make
recommendations for the future strengthening of the electoral process. (All Africa)

                                       WESTERN HEMISPHERE
BRAZIL
The environment ministry increased its measurement of the amount of Amazon rainforest destroyed between
August 2009 and July 2010 to 2703 square miles. This is the lowest annual figure since tracking began two
decades ago. (Miami Herald)

COLOMBIA
Three members of a Front of the Revolutionary Armed Forces (FARC) unit died fighting the Colombian navy at
the mouth of the San Juan river. Another six members are missing after the boat they were defending burned
and sank. (Latin American Herald Tribune)

                                       EAST ASIA AND PACIFIC
PHILIPPINES
President Aquino said the government's disaster response needs improvement, including stronger evacuation
alerts and the development of a rehabilitation plan for victims of recent typhoons. (The Philippine Star)

DEMOCRATIC PEOPLE'S REPUBLIC OF KOREA/JAPAN/THE REPUBLIC OF KOREA
Nine defectors from the DPRK who have been under protective custody in Japan since September 13 are
expected to fly to Seoul October 4. The group, which includes three children, will be sent to a state-operated
temporary base for DPRK defectors. (Yonhap News Agency)

                                                   EUROPE
UNITED KINGDOM/BANGLADESH
The UK announced it will double aid to Bangladesh in next four years and continue to be the country's largest
bilateral donor. The aid will prioritize education and technical skills development. (Xinhua News)

RUSSIA/INDIA
The Russian and Indian defense ministers are scheduled to meet in Moscow October 4 to discuss the
intergovernmental commission on military-technical cooperation. (RL4 Novosti)
