                                                MIDDLE EAST
SYRIA
The Organization of Islamic Cooperation warned Syria about the consequences of its continued use of force
against anti-regime protesters and said in an October 11 statement "the use of force will only lead to more
violence and bloodshed, thus exacerbating the crisis and making it more complex." (The Daily Times)

EGYPT
Prime Minister Sharaf said October 11 his government would only resign at the orders of the ruling military
council, amid mounting anger over the government's handling of deadly protests in Cairo late Sunday which led
to the death of 25 Coptic Christians. (The Daily Star)

                                      SOUTH AND CENTRAL ASIA
AFGHANISTAN/PAKISTAN
Pakistani Prime Minister Gilani offered intelligence to Afghanistan related to the investigation of former
Afghan President Rabbani's assassination. (Dawn)

INDIA/TURKEY
Counterterrorism efforts led the agenda during bilateral October 11 talks between India and Turkey, as did the
Indian vote on the UNSCR. (The Hindu)

                                                    EUROPE
FRANCE/TURKEY
Turkey's ruling and opposition parties united in criticizing French President Sarkozy's threat to revive a bill
criminalizing any denial of Armenian genocide claims. (Hurriyet Daily News)

RUSSIA/EUROPEAN UNION
A group of Duma deputies appealed to the European Parliament to drop visa requirements for Russians who fly
frequently into the European Union on return airline tickets. (The Moscow Times)

                                        EAST ASIA AND PACIFIC
THE REPUBLIC OF KOREA/DEMOCRATIC PEOPLE'S REPUBLIC OF KOREA
Sohn Halc-kyu, the leader of South Korea's main opposition Democratic Party, called October 12 for the
government to improve ties with the North as Pyongyang issued a warning against propaganda leaflets sent
across the border by activists. (Yonhap News Agency)

CHINA/VIETNAM
The two countries signed an agreement October 11 on basic principles to settle maritime issues and resolve
territorial and border issues. (Xinhua News)

                                                    AFRICA
KENYA
While the Kenyan shilling slid to its lowest value ever against the U.S. dollar and lost a third of its value in
2011, the head of the Central Bank argued his agency had done all it could within its mandate by increasing
interest rates. In his testimony before a parliamentary committee, Governor Ndung'u said other government
agencies, including the Treasury, would now need to focus fiscal policies to implement necessary
measures. (Nation)

ZIMBABWE
President Mugabe's failing health has possibly forced his Zanu-PF party to press for early elections and
accelerated a plan compelling foreign firms to surrender majority shares to the government. Despite this, he has
not yet loosened his grip on power. (News24)

                                        WESTERN HEMISPHERE
CANADA/LIBYA
Canadian Foreign Minister Baird traveled to Libya to discuss arms control with the TNC and was accompanied
by several executives from large Canadian companies including Montreal engineering giant SNC-Lavalin,
Alberta oil giant Suncor, and Calgary based pipeline technology firm Pure Technologies in an effort to promote
Canadian business interests with Libya's post-Qadhafi rulers. (The Globe and Mail)

BRAZIL
President Rousseff proposed expanding financial assistance to include 17 million more families living in
conditions of extreme poverty. The President answered critics denying the expansion of financial aid would
boost the nation's birthrate. (Latin American Herald Tribune)
